3.8 Confidence Intervals for Population Proportions



Confidence Intervals: Proportions



When constructing a confidence interval, we use data from a sample to estimate information about the population. With large enough samples, we can use the binomial theorem to approximate a continuous normal distribution and therefore use z-scores when constructing the confidence interval.




Requirements:

  • Random Sample
  • Binomial Distribution: Fixed n, Independent trials, 2 outcomes, Constant Probability
  • Number of successes \((n \widehat{p})\) and number of failures \((n \widehat{q})\) are both greater than 5



Best Point Estimate

The proportion of your sample, \(\widehat p\)



Margin of Error

\(E=z_\frac\alpha2\left(\sqrt{\frac{\displaystyle\widehat p\widehat q}n}\right)\)

  1. For a class project, a political science student at a large university wants to estimate the percent of students who are registered voters. The student surveys 500 students and finds that 300 are registered voters.
    1. What is the best point estimate of the population proportion of students who are registered voters?

      \(\hat{p}=\frac{x}{n}=\frac{300}{500}=0.60\)

    2. Use the sample data to construct a 90% confidence interval estimate of the percentage of students who are registered voters.

      (0.5640, 0.6360)

    3. What is the margin of error for this confidence interval?

      \(0.6-0.5640=0.036\)

      The margin of error is 0.036.

    4. Write the confidence interval in three ways.

      \(.6000 \pm .0360\)

      (0.5640, 0.6360)

      \(0.5640 < p < 0.6360\)

    5. Based on the results, are a majority of students registered to vote? Why or why not?

      We can be 90% confident that the majority of students are registered to vote since the minimum value of the confidence interval is greater than 50%.

  2. The EPA considers indoor radon levels above 4 picocuries per liter (pCi/L) of air to be high enough to warrant amelioration efforts. Tests in a sample of 200 Centre County Pennsylvania homes found 63.5% of these sampled households to have indoor radon levels above 4 pCi/L.
    1. What is the best point estimate of the population proportion of households with indoor radon levels above 4 pCi/L?

      \(\hat{p}=0.635\)

    2. Use the sample data to construct a 95% confidence interval estimate of the percentage of students who are registered voters.

      (0.5683, 0.7017)

    3. What is the margin of error for this confidence interval?

      \(0.635-0.5683=0.0667\)

      The margin of error is 0.0667.

    4. Write the confidence interval in three ways.

      \(.635 \pm .0667\)

      (0.5683, 0.7017)

      \(0.5683 < p < 0.7017\)

  3. In the margin of error formula used to calculate confidence intervals for proportions, \(z_\frac\alpha2\), is called the critical value.
    1. Find the critical value for a 90% level of confidence.

      1.645

    2. Find the critical value for a 99% level of confidence.

      2.575

  7. Suppose an internet marketing company wants to determine the current percentage of customers who click on ads on their smartphones. How many customers should the company survey in order to be 90% confident that the estimated proportion is within five percentage points of the true population proportion of customers who click on ads on their smartphones?     OpenStax

    • Use Stat Crunch: Stats ~ Proportions ~ One Sample ~ Width/Sample size

      We do not know \( \hat{p} \) so we will have to assume \( \hat{p} \) = .50

      The width of the confidence interval is twice the margin of error.

      CL=90%, Target proportion = 0.50, width = 0.10


    • OR Use the formula \(n=\widehat p\widehat q\left(\frac{z_{\displaystyle\frac\alpha2}}E\right)^2\)

      We do not know \( \hat{p} \) so we will have to assume \( \hat{p} \) = .50

      CL = 90%, \(z_\frac\alpha2=1.645\), E = .05, \( \hat{p}== .50 \), \( \hat{q}== .50 \), n = 271 adults

    In order to be 90% confident our sample proportion is within 5% of the actual population proportion. We need to survey 271 adults.
