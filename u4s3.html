4.3 Hypothesis Testing for Means

Realistically, if we are testing a claim about the mean of a population, we do not know the standard deviation of the population. Therefore, we must use the student t-distribution when hypothesis testing for means.



  1. Changing a Tire: The length of time to change a tire is normally distributed. A random sample of ten tire changes is taken with a mean of 22.63 minutes and a standard deviation of 2.98 minutes. Use a 0.05 significance level to test the claim that the mean time to change a tire is less than 25 minutes.
    1. The original Claim: \(\mu < 25\)
    2. \(H_0\): \(\mu \geq 25\)
    3. \(H_A\): \(\mu < 25\)
    4. Type of Test: Left-tailed test
    5. Level of Significance: \(\alpha =.05\)
    6. Degrees of Freedom (n-1): \(10-1=9\)
    7. Critical Value(s) (use T calculator in Stat Crunch with area \(\alpha\) ):: \(t=-1.8331\)

    8. Test Statistic:

      \(t=\frac{\overline{x}-\mu}{\left(\frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}\right)}=\frac{22.63-25}{\left(\frac{2.98}{\sqrt{10}}\right)}=-2.5150\)

    9. p-value: 0.0165
    10. Decision about the null: Reject \(H_0\)

      Reason 1: 0.0165 < .05 or Reason 2: -2.5150 is in the rejection region

    11. Concluding statement about the claim: There is sufficient evidence to support the claim the mean time to change a tire is less than 25 minutes.

  2. Age of Cars: The mean age of cars driven by Pellissippi State students is seven years. The Dean of Students claims that this is an accurate statement for his students. A random sample of 31 cars in the East Parking Lot on campus showed a mean age of 8.1 years with a standard deviation of 5.1 years. Test the Dean’s claim at the 0.01 significance level.
    1. The original Claim: \(\mu = 7\)
    2. \(H_0\): \(\mu = 7\)
    3. \(H_A\): \(\mu \neq 7\)
    4. Type of Test: Two-tailed test
    5. Level of Significance: \(\alpha =.01\)
    6. Degrees of Freedom: \(31-1=30\)
    7. Critical Value(s): \(t=\pm 2.750\) Determines shaded area(s)

    8. Test Statistic:

      \(\mathrm{t}=\frac{\overline{x}-\mu}{\left(\frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}\right)}=\frac{8.1-7}{\left(\frac{5.1}{\sqrt{31}}\right)}=1.20089\)

    9. p-value: 0.2392
    10. Decision about the null: Fail to reject \(H_0\)

      Reason 1: 0.2392 > .01

      Reason 2: 1.20089 is not in the rejection region
    11. Concluding statement about the claim: There is not sufficient evidence to reject the claim the mean age of cars driven by PSCC students is 7 years.

  3. Class Size: You receive a brochure from a large university. The brochure indicates that the mean class size for full-time faculty is fewer than 31 students. You want to test this claim. You randomly select 18 classes taught by full-time faculty and determine the class size of each. The results are shown in the table below. At a 0.01 significance level, can you support the university’s claim?
    34 26 28 33 32 37 28 25 29
    26 28 37 30 31 25 29 31 28
    Sample Statistics: \(\overline{x}=29.8333333 \quad s=3.666221 \quad n=18\)
    1. The original Claim: \(\mu < 31\)
    2. \(H_0\): \(\mu \geq 31\)
    3. \(H_A\): \(\mu < 31\)
    4. Type of Test: Left-tailed test
    5. Level of Significance: \(\alpha =0.01\)
    6. Degrees of Freedom: \(18-1=17\)
    7. Critical Value(s): \(t=-2.5669\) Determines shaded area(s)

    8. Test Statistic:

      \(t =\frac{\overline{x}-\mu}{\left(\frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}\right)} = \frac{29.833333-31}{\left(\frac{3.66621}{\sqrt{18}}\right)}=-1.3501\)

    9. p-value: 0.0973
    10. Decision about the null: Fail to reject \(H_0\)

      Reason 1: 0.0973 > .01

      Reason 2: -1.3051 is not in the rejection region

    11. Concluding statement about the claim: There is not sufficient evidence to support the claim the mean class size for full time faculty is fewer than 31 students.
