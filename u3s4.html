3.4 Perimeter and Area

  1. The figure is drawn on centimeter graph paper.
    1. Find the perimeter of the figure. \(P=7+6+3+2+4+4=26   cm\)
    2. Find the area of the figure. \(A=l \times w\)

      Larger rectangle: \(A=7 \times 4 = 28 cm^2\)

      Smaller rectangle: \(A=3 \times 2 = 6 cm^2\)

      Total area: \(A=28 + 6= 34 cm^2\)

  2. Find the area of the triangle. The triangle is drawn on centimeter graph paper. \(A=\frac12 \times b \times h\)

    \(A=\frac12 \times 6 \times 4 = 12 cm^2\)



  3. Find the volume of the box (rectangular prism).

    \(V=l \times w \times h\)

    \(V=4 \times 2 \times 10 = 80 cm^3\)



  4. The circle is drawn on centimeter graph paper.
    1. Find the circumference of the circle using a formula. \(C= \pi \times d\)

      \(C= \pi \times 8 \approx 25.13 cm\)

    2. Approximate the area of the circle by counting squares inside the circle.

      Approximately \( 50\) or \(51 cm^2\)

    3. Find the area of the circle using a formula. \(A= \pi \times r^2\)

      \(C= \pi \times 4^2 \approx 50.27 cm^2\)

  5. A bell-shaped curve is drawn above a number line.
    1. Approximate the area in square units underneath the curve (and above the number line). Approximately \( 32 cm^2\)
    2. Approximate the area underneath the curve to the left of the vertical line. Approximately \( 11 cm^2\)
    3. Approximately what percentage of the area underneath the curve is to the left of the vertical line? \( 11/32 \approx 0.3438 = 34.38\%\)


  6. Your backyard currently has no grass. You want to seed your backyard and enclose it with a fence for your dog.
    1. Determine the perimeter of your backyard.
      2. \(24+12+9+9+15+21=90 ft\)
    2. If chain link fencing costs $14 per foot installed, how much will it cost to enclose your backyard?
      3. \(90 \times 14 = $1260\)
    3. Determine the area of your backyard.

      4. \(24 \times 12 + 15 \times 9 = 423\; ft^2\)

    4. If the cost of Kentucky blue grass seed is $0.03 for 1 square foot, how much will the seed for the backyard cost?
      5. \(423 \times 0.03 = $12.69\)
  7. You have a rectangular driveway with a circular garden in the middle of it.

    1. The circular garden has a radius of 2 yards. Determine the area of the garden.
      2. \( A=\pi r^{2} \)

      \( A=\pi \times 2^2 \)

      The area of the garden is 12.57 square yards.

    2. Your driveway is cracked and needs repaving. Calculate the area of the driveway that would be covered with new asphalt.

      3. The area of the garden is 12.57 square yards

      The area of the entire driveway is \(A=lw=19 \times 6 = 114\; yds^2\)

      \(114-12.57=101.43\)

      The area to be covered by the new asphalt is 101.43 square yards.

    3. If it costs $20.70 to repave each square yard with asphalt, then how much would you pay to repave your driveway?
      4. \(101.43 \times $20.70 = $2099.60\)

  10. Addison wants to replace the old vinyl flooring in her living room and hallway with wood flooring. The dimensions of her hallway and living room floors are shown in the diagram.

    1. Find the total area that will be replaced with wood flooring.

      2. \(A = 3.75 \times 9.5 + 16.5 \times 15 = 283.125ft^2\)

    2. The planks in each box of wood flooring at the store will cover an area of 40 square feet. How many boxes does Addison need to buy?

  11. You want to give the front of your garage a new coat of paint. All of the siding needs to be painted except for the garage door. The door is 7 feet high and 16 feet wide. How many square feet of siding will you be painting?

    Area to be painted = Area of rectangle + Area of triangle - Area of door

    Area of rectangle: \(A = l \times w = 26 \times 10.5=273 ft^2\)

    Area of triangle: \(A=\frac12 \times b \times h = \frac12 \times 26 \times 4.75 = 61.75 ft^2\)

    Area of door: \(A= l \times w = 7 \times 16 = 112 ft^2\)

    Area to be painted = \(273 ft^2 + 61.75 ft^2 - 112 ft^2 = 222.75 ft^2\)
